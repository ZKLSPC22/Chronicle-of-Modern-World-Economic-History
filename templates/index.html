Chronicle of World Economical-History

by Zhikun Li

As a STEM student, I find readings in humanities to fall into two categories: overly dumbed down, broad, and filled with subjective claims, or too sophisticated and focused on small parts of the bigger picture. Indeed, these are pseudo-problems stemming from my impossible wish to learn unbiased history while putting in little time. However, I still believe that the process of striving towards an impossible goal itself is meaningful.

For the above reason, I decided to create this chronicle of world economic history, which is a collection of my notes on the history of economics, written to contain facts and figures, leaving the conclusion to be made by the reader.

I hope that this chronicle can help me to understand the world better, and I hope that it can help you to understand the world better as well.
